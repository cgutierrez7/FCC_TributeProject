Satoru Iwata

"On my business card, I am a corporate president. In my mind, I am a game developer. But in my heart, I am a gamer."

Satoru Iwata making announcements at the Game Developers Conference in San Francisco, California. March 2, 2011.

Career in Video Games

  • 1980 - Becomes a part time programmer at HAL Laboratory
  • 1982 - Graduates university and gets promoted to full-time employee as the only programmer at HAL Laboratory
  • 1993 - Becomes the president of HAL Laboratory during financial decline and stabilizes the company.
  • 2000 - Employed by Nintendo as head of the coporate planning division and a seat on board of directors
  • 2002 - Promoted to president of Nintendo
  • 2003 - Begins discussions in the company about new, revolutionary, gaming console
  • 2006 - The Nintendo Wii, launches under Iwata's leadership
  • 2009 - The success of the Wii leads to record sales and profits for Nintendo under Iwata's leadership
  • 2011-12 - Nintendo 3DS and Wii U launch to lackluster sales, leading to operating losses, and Iwata halving his salary as an apology for his performance
  • 2013 - Takes on role of CEO and develops concept which leads to creation of Amiibo
  • 2014 - Has surgery to remove a tumor in his bile duct
  • 2015 - After multiple bouts of sickness, on July 11 Iwata passed away due to complications from his tumor at the age of 55
